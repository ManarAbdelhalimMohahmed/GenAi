Generative AI

  • Has become a prominent buzzword in tech circles
  • A subset of artificial intelligence, which itself is:
    • A scientific discipline
    • A branch of computer science focused on creating intelligent agents
    • Systems capable of reasoning, learning, and autonomous decision-making
Slide 3 of 13

Machine Learning (ML)

  • A subfield of AI where systems:
    • Train models using input data
    • Make predictions on new, unseen data from similar sources
  • Key characteristic: Enables computers to learn without explicit programming
Slide 4 of 13

ML Model Types

  • Supervised Learning:
    • Uses labeled data (tagged with names/types/numbers)
    • Learns from historical examples to predict future values
    • Example: Predicting tip amounts based on bill totals
  • Unsupervised Learning:
    • Works with unlabeled data
    • Discovers natural groupings/patterns in raw data
Slide 5 of 13

Deep Learning

  • An advanced ML approach using:
    • Artificial Neural Networks (inspired by human brain structure)
    • Composed of interconnected neurons that process data to make predictions
  • Capable of handling more complex patterns than traditional ML
  • Supports semi-supervised learning:
    • Combines small labeled datasets with large unlabeled ones
    • Enhances model generalization
Slide 6 of 13

Generative AI Fundamentals

  • A deep learning subset leveraging neural networks
  • Processes both labeled and unlabeled data
  • Utilizes supervised, unsupervised, and semi-supervised methods
Slide 7 of 13

Model Categories

  • Discriminative Models:
    • Classify/predict labels for data points
    • Learn relationships between features and labels
    • Example: Distinguishing cats from dogs
  • Generative Models:
    • Create new data instances based on learned probability distributions
    • Learn joint probability distributions (P(x,y))
    • Example: Generating original dog images
Slide 8 of 13

GenAI Output Criteria

  • Is GenAI: Natural language, audio, images, video
  • Not GenAI: Numbers, class labels (e.g., "spam"), probabilities
Slide 9 of 13

Foundation Models

  • Large-scale AI models pre-trained on vast datasets
  • Adaptable to diverse downstream tasks:
    • Sentiment analysis
    • Image captioning
    • Fraud detection
    • Customer support automation
  • Transformative potential across industries (healthcare, finance, etc.)
Slide 10 of 13

GenAI Applications

  • Text-to-Text: Language translation
  • Text-to-Image: Image generation from descriptions (e.g., diffusion models)
  • Text-to-Video: Video creation from scripts
  • Text-to-Task: Action execution based on text input (e.g., GUI navigation)
